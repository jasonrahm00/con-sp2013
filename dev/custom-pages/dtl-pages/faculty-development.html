The Faculty Development team’s mission is to support all faculty by providing resources and services to enrich teaching, enhance student learning, facilitate scholarship in teaching and learning, and promote collaboration in a collegial environment.

The Faculty Development Team is the in-house expert for mentoring and best practices in design, evaluation, teaching, and learning.

Come visit any time! The Faculty Development Team is always working on something fun and engaging for faculty.

Who We Are

Candace Berardinelli PhD, RN, Director of Faculty Development

Candace goes by Candy, but her grandkids call her Nonni. She spends most of her time being an Associate Professor and the Director of Faculty Development in the University of Colorado College of Nursing. If asked what she does in her free time, Candy’s answer is, “Free time? I haven’t had that since before I was a nurse!” But, she will admit she enjoys walking and reading when she has a spare moment.

In 2013, Candy decided it was time for her to join the CON faculty. Shortly after she arrived, was elected as UCEC chair. She served as chair of UCEC from 2013-2015. But, Candy’s story of CON service and leadership doesn’t end in 2015. Since 2015, she has served as a member of APT representing the non-tenure track faculty. She has also served on numerous task forces and search committees.

Candy enjoys her time with faculty along with her time with students. Candy teaches online courses in the nursing RN-BS undergraduate and the education certificate programs. And as for time with faculty, she develops and implements faculty development opportunities for CON faculty as Director of Faculty Development.

Candy is always available for visits, phone calls, and sharing resources. In fact, she loves her faculty development work so much she is currently an advisory board member for the Colorado Collaborative for Preparing Future Faculty Network. Candy is an expert and community leader for faculty development.

Candy has a rich educational background accompanied by many years of experience in academics and nursing. Dr. Berardinelli earned her BSN at the University of Pittsburgh and both her MS/ANP and PhD in nursing at the University of Colorado College of Nursing.

Examples of Faculty Development Work

Individual/Group Consultation

The consultations may be one-on-one conversations or group workshops, informal or structured.

  • Topics by Appointment
    • Curriculum/Course Design
    • Peer Review
    • Assessment of Teaching
    • Interpreting Student and Course Evaluations
  • Topics by Walk-In, Email, or Phone
    • Time Management
    • Formative Assessment
    • Strategies for Teaching
    • Interpretation of Student Evaluations
    • Share Ideas or Concerns Related to Teaching Roles

Faculty Reading Circle

Faculty meets over a semester to read and discuss a book or series of articles that encourage professional development, new ideas, etc.

New Faculty Orientation and Mentoring

An online orientation to the faculty role, accompanied by support from the director of faculty development, is offered to assist new colleagues in acclimating to life and teaching in the CON. Plans are to include mentoring over the first and second year of teaching with experienced colleagues.

Brown-Bag “Talking Teaching” Sessions

CON faculty are invited to lead and/or participate in one hour-long sessions on topics that encourage teaching excellence, innovative teaching strategies, successful student learning initiatives, and “hot topics” related to teaching or learning. Faculty are welcome to suggest topics of interest. Some sessions will be “open” forums for dialogue related to teaching and learning.
