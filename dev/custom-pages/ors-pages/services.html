Services and Functions

Pre-Award

The ORS Pre-Award Specialist can assist with the following:

  • Funding opportunities (i.e., searching internet for appropriate research funding opportunities and sending to faculty per their research areas of interest
  • Preparing and submitting grant applications (i.e., preparing budgets, drafting budget narratives, serving as liaison with external contacts for all subcontracts, reviewing biosketches, reviewing documents for consistency and formatting, ensuring adherence to sponsor guidelines)
  • Routing sponsored project applications to Office of Grants and Contracts
  • Maintaining grant submission and funding data
  • Entering and maintaining data in the Graduate School database and serving as liaison with CON staff for table data for training grants (Ts and Ps)
  • Keep researching faculty updated on sponsor changes and shifts in policy/procedure

Post-Award

The ORS Post-Award Specialist assists Principal Investigators (PIs) and Departmental Administrators with the coordination/administration of research grants, contracts, and procurement policies. Specific areas of responsibility include the following:

  • Post-award budgeting, progress reports, project close-outs, strategic planning, and project activities to support the research mission of the College
  • Reviewing and approving, as appropriate, sub-recipient invoices related to the project, ensuring compliance with sponsor cost principles
  • Maintaining CON Post-Award grant archives in accordance with University’s and CON’s archive policies, recommending revisions and changes as needed
  • Serving as an alternate to the Pre-Award Grants Coordinator for grant application submissions
  • Performing work and special projects as outlined and directed by the Associate Dean/ORS and the CON Dean
  • Serving as a resource and advisor to common research interests with the Children’s Hospital, Denver Health Medical Center, VA Hospital, and any other institution desiring collaboration with the College of Nursing
  • Assisting in the coordination of purchasing activities, including purchase orders, standing purchase orders, payment vouchers, utilization of State credit card within the parameters of relevant fiscal rules, and purchasing policy
  • Working collaboratively with ORS staff to assist with department activities and special events related to research efforts
  • Coordinating with the Budget & Finance Manager to provide fiscal management support to the CON Budget and Finance Office of designated sponsored research services and training accounts, with particular attention to deficit monitoring
  • Collaborating with OGC Accounting, subcontractors, and external agencies, regarding payment of invoices, requesting no-cost extensions, establishing subcontracts, and assisting with progress reports
  • Attending required training and other seminars to further develop sponsored project fiscal management skills
  • Maintaining a log of intramural data for faculty, using Excel, Access, or other database software
  • Acting as a resource to Sheridan Health Services sponsored projects
  • Advising Principal Investigators on financial management and compliance issues and serve as liaison to sponsors over the term of the project
  • Providing ongoing support, monitoring, and maintenance of ORS accounts for the production of complete monthly budget and develop special reports as needed
